Benchmarks vs. Evals vs. Tests: Three Different Instruments

Using the wrong instrument for the job is the most common mistake in AI evaluation
Benchmark
Purpose
Cross-model comparison on standardized tasks
Data
Fixed, public or semi-public dataset
Scope
Model capabilities in isolation
Frequency
Point-in-time, periodic
Ownership
Research community, academic labs
Lifecycle
Long; infrequent versioned updates
Analogy
Blood pressure screening
Eval Set
Purpose
Validate production behavior for your use case
Data
Domain-specific, often proprietary
Scope
System-level (RAG, tools, multi-turn)
Frequency
Continuous, in CI/CD
Ownership
Product / engineering team
Lifecycle
Short; frequently refreshed
Analogy
Diagnostic workup
Test
Purpose
Guard against regressions in deployed systems
Data
Golden datasets from production failures
Scope
Specific behaviors, edge cases, safety
Frequency
On every code change
Ownership
QA / engineering team
Lifecycle
Continuous; grows from production failures
Analogy
Daily vital signs
Taxonomy adapted from Thoughtworks, "LLM benchmarks, evals and tests" (2024); Anthropic, "Demystifying evals for AI agents" (2026); Hamel Husain, "Your AI Product Needs Evals" (2024).
